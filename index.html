Portfolio Reflection

What I've learned this semester about writing, revision, and myself as a writer.

What I Learned This Semester

[Replace this with your own text.] The biggest thing I learned is that revision is not punishment — it's an opportunity to think more deeply about what I was trying to say. My first drafts are almost always unclear because I'm still figuring out my own position as I write. By the third or fourth draft, I've usually found the thing that actually matters.

I also learned that I am more patient than I gave myself credit for. The research paper required staying with a topic for weeks without being sure where I was going. I expected to hate that uncertainty. Instead, I found that it is where the most interesting thinking happens.

Most importantly, I learned that I write to understand — not to report. When I finished an assignment and felt satisfied, it was always because I had figured something out that I didn't know when I started. That is the kind of writer I want to keep being.
